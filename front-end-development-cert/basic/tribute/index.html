Barney Kessel - Guitar Wizard


The man of a thousand chords.


A swinging jazz guitar master in his natural habitat - pleasing the crowds.

Barney Kessel was a legendary jazz guitarist known for his rhythmic drive and his gentle personality. Like many of his generation, he grew up listening to Charlie Christian recordings, learning an economy of notes and an infectious sense of swing. Far from being a hinderence, growing up in Oklahoma meant that he got to play with any jazz talent passing though. Kessel was one of the founders of the bebop guitar school and went on to develop a sophisticated chordal vocabulary that was somehow both pianistic and guitaristic. He is remembered for countless jazz recordings, movie soundtracks, pop albums (as a member of the legendary "Wrecking Crew"), and instructional materials. To the general public he is probably best known as the guitarist on Julie London's sultry, hit recording of Cry Me a River.

Timeline of Barney's life:

  • 1923 – Born in Muskogee, OK
  • 1937 – At age 14, leaves school and begins playing professionally in local dance bands
  • 1937 – Begins playing in Ellis Ezeli's band, being the only white kid in an all black band playing local black clubs
  • 1939 – Joins the Oklahoma A&M band, "Hal Proce and the Varsitonians"
  • 1942 – Guitar legend and fellow Oklahoman Charlie Christian urges Barney to move to Los Angeles
  • 1943 – Plays in Chico Marx's band
  • 1944 – In the film Jammin' the Blues with Lester Young
  • 1946 – Tours and records with Benny Goodman
  • 1947 – Records with Charlie Parker in the Relaxin' at Camarillo sessions
  • 1952 – Joins the Oscar Peterson Trio
  • 1955 – Records legendary Cry Me a River on with Julie London on Julie Is Her Name
  • 1956 – Voted "Best Guitarist" in Down Beat's Readers Poll, for four years straight
  • 1957 – Records The Poll Winners with fellow Down Beat Readers Poll winners Ray Brown and Shelley Manne, the first of three such albums
  • 1992 – Passes away of a brain tumor at his home in San Diego, CA
